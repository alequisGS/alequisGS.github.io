About

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at PUC-Rio, working under the supervision of Sergey Galkin. My research lies at the interface of algebraic geometry, Homological Mirror Symmetry, derived categories, Fano and log del Pezzo geometry, Landau–Ginzburg models, periods, monodromy, and arithmetic aspects of mirror symmetry.

Current project

My current work studies the degree-10 weighted hypersurface X10 ⊂ ℙ(1,2,3,5), a log del Pezzo surface of index one with a quotient singularity of type 1/3(1,1). I am interested in its birational geometry, minimal resolution, canonical Deligne–Mumford stack, semiorthogonal decompositions, exceptional collections, and mirror Landau–Ginzburg models.

The associated paper proves a full exceptional collection result for canonical stacks of complex log del Pezzo surfaces with 1/3(1,1)-singularities, and specializes the construction to X10.

Thesis project and fellowship

I was awarded the FAPERJ Nota 10 Excellence Scholarship for my PhD thesis project, Functional Equations for Landau–Ginzburg Models and Exponential Motives . The project studies graph potentials, exponential integrals and sums, Kloosterman-type phenomena, trace formulas, and arithmetic realizations of mirror-symmetric structures.
